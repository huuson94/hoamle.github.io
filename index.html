I am a research assistant/student at the Data Science Lab, Hanoi University of Science & Technology (DSLab-HUST), reading and working on statistical machine learning. My research interests include deep neural networks and latent variable models, with focus on informative deep probabilistic models that endorse interpretability and scalability for downstream applications in other domains (e.g. computer vision, bioinformatics, recommendation). I am also particularly interested in the advances of conditional generative image modelling - a branch of generative models which has contributed ample insights to representation learning, in addition with inspiring applications in vision/graphics.

Prior to HUST, I had a brief but wonderful time at FPT Software Hanoi. In my early academic journey, I was a summer intern at Dr Le Novere's lab, also completed an M.Phil. in Computational Biology at Cambridge University (2014), and a B.Sc. in Mathematics with Finance at Exeter University (2013). Before that, I was a B.A. student at the Business school, National Economics University for one year.
